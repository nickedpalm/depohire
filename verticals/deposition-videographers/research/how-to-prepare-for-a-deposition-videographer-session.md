     8→### Industry Standards and Best Practices
     9→**Attorney's Pre-Deposition Checklist**
    10→- **Witness Preparation (7 Core Tips):** Teach upright posture (feet flat, hands on table/lap, no slouching); neutral non-verbal cues; clear verbal skills; practice "golden rules" (listen fully, pause, answer only asked question, say "I don't know/recall" if true); mock video sessions for body language review[1][4][6].
    11→- **Room Setup Requirements:**
    12→  | Element | Standards |
    13→  |---------|-----------|
    14→  | Space | Ample floor space for video equipment; videographer movement; avoid crowded tables[3]. |
    15→  | Background | Neutral, plain wall or solid subdued color; no artwork, windows, distractions[1]. |
    16→  | Lighting | Even, diffused, soft; no shadows, glare, or washout; maximize color balance; pre-visit room[1][2][4]. |
    17→  | Audio | Quiet room (no traffic, HVAC, phones, fans); proper mic placement/testing for all speakers (attorney + witness); backup audio recording[1][2][6]. |
    18→  | Equipment | High focal length camera lens; standard play speed; tripod (e.g., Dolica 65"); lavalier/shotgun mics; headphones for monitoring; extra memory cards/batteries; laptop (e.g., MacBook Pro) for file management; full backups (video/audio independent streams)[2][5][7]. |
    19→- **Technical Checks:** Test all gear (cameras, mics, internet for remote) pre-deposition; backups mandatory; 24-48 hour full rehearsal with all participants[1][4][7].
    20→- **Videographer Obligations:** Caption video (matter, location, parties); high-quality non-webcam recording; test recordings[2][7].
    21→- **Video Optimization:** Witness camera-facing neutral/smile at start for edits; even audio levels; control noise[6].
    22→- **Remote Additions:** Secondary internet (hotspot), backup power, dial-in audio, one-page tech guides with IT contact[4].
