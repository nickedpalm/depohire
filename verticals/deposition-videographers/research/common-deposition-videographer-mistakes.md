     9→### 3. Industry Standards and Best Practices
    10→- **Hire legal videographer routinely**: Video captures non-verbal cues (e.g., demeanor) critical for jury impact; written transcripts insufficient. Video depositions admissible "for any purpose" under rules like Nevada Rule 32, including non-impeachment use[1][2].
    11→- **Ask succinct, targeted questions**: Avoid open-ended queries allowing evasion; repeat questions relentlessly for "sound bites" despite "asked/answered" objections[1][3].
    12→- **Schedule ample time**: Minimum **1 hour** per deposition; factor in rephrasing for evasive answers[1].
    13→- **Observe demeanor**: Train to note body language shifts (nervous, evasive); video preserves for trial[2][6].
    14→- **Prepare thoroughly**: Outline questions, anticipate counters; coach witnesses for concise, honest responses[6].
    15→- **Handle disruptions**: Use referees for Zoom/off-camera issues; object aggressively to video misuse at trial[4].
