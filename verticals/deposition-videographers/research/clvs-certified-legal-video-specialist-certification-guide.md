     1→### CLVS Certification Overview
     2→Certified Legal Video Specialist (CLVS) is an NCRA credential for legal videographers demonstrating proficiency in video deposition capture, utilization, retention, and partnership with court reporters to maintain record integrity.[2] No federal, state, or local licensing is required for legal videographers; CLVS is voluntary from professional associations like NCRA.[3]
     3→
     4→### Requirements and How to Obtain
     5→- **Mandatory First Step**: Complete online CLVS Education workshop (7 modules on deposition recording).[2][5]
     6→- **Production Exam**: Hands-on staging of a mock deposition at NCRA HQ (Reston, VA); graded on guidelines adherence and video quality. Upcoming: Hands-on training April 30, 2026; exam May 1–2, 2026. Record valid for 3 years; must pass Written Knowledge Test (WKT) within that period or retake.[2]
     7→- **Written Knowledge Test (WKT)**: Based on education materials; can follow production exam.[2]
     8→- **Education/Training**: >2 years post-high school education/training recommended; oral/written exams required.[4] No degree mandatory; backgrounds in film production, legal studies, or communications advantageous.[1]
     9→- **Renewal**: 1.0 CEU (10 hours approved seminars) every 3-year cycle; 0.25 PDCs per passed exam for holders of other NCRA certs.[2]
    10→- **Ethics**: Adhere to voluntary Principles for Professional Practice plus local/state/federal rules.[2]
    11→- **Full Process Cost Estimate**: $1,500–$2,000 including training, workshop, exam (members lower).[1] Specifics: Production exam members $325/non $425; optional hands-on training members $200/non $300; WKT members $200/non $300.[2][5]

    13→### What It Means
