    16→### 3. Industry Standards and Best Practices
    17→- **Certification**: Certified Legal Video Specialist (CLVS) from NCRA required for proficiency in video deposition capture, utilization, retention; mandatory 7-module online workshop before exams; partners with court reporters for transcript-video integrity.[7]
    18→- **Equipment/Process**: High-quality video mandatory (no amateurism tolerated in 2026 courts); includes pre-production, production, post-production; synchronized video-transcript delivery.[2][3][7][8]
    19→- **Remote Adoption**: Use certified videographers for virtual setups to ensure quality; all-inclusive remote packages reduce travel/venue costs.[5]
    20→- **Hiring**: Local videographers for cost savings, but coordinate with court reporters/witnesses; direct hires by attorneys/paralegals possible.[4][5]
