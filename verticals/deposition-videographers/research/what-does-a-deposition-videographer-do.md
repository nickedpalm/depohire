    11→### 3. Industry Standards and Best Practices
    12→- **Core responsibilities**: Travel to deposition sites (e.g., Bay Area offices), set up/test professional audio/video equipment, record proceedings unobtrusively, capture non-verbal cues (facial expressions, pauses, body language), announce "on/off record," perform legal read-on/off, troubleshoot technical issues[1][2][3][5][6].
    13→- **Post-production**: Edit recordings with voice-overs for witness ID/exam explanations, sync video to transcripts, create impeachment clips, provide formats like synchronized transcripts, Picture-in-Picture (PIP), closed captioning[3][4][6].
    14→- **Certification**: NCRA CLVS requires videography skills (equipment/software mastery, e.g., Final Cut Pro, Adobe Premiere Pro), written exam, production test (mock deposition)[4][9].
    15→- **Arrival protocol**: Arrive early, handle all technical setup without disrupting proceedings[6].
    16→- **Equipment**: Single-camera setups common; partner with court reporters for transcript-video integrity[1][7][9].
    17→- **No prior experience needed** for entry-level; basic video/audio knowledge ideal[1].
