    10→### 3. Industry Standards and Best Practices
    11→- **Certification and Oath Authority**: Videographers must be authorized to administer oath in some states (e.g., notary, court reporter, bailiff); CLVS certification via NCRA enforces competency standards—verify beyond self-advertised "certified" status[1][5][6].
    12→- **Federal Rules Compliance (Rule 30)**: No video manipulation, filters, or distortions; must include court reporter's opening (name, address, location, date, time, oath, attendees); deponent/attorney demeanor undistorted[2].
    13→- **Technical Setup**: Professional sound (check mics), lighting (no shadows/distortions), camera framing (witness focal point), distraction-free environment; secure encrypted storage for videos/transcripts[4].
    14→- **Post-Production**: Synchronize video with transcript for simultaneous viewing; prepare for courtroom playback[5].
    15→- **Etiquette/Arrival**: Arrive early for equipment setup and room logistics[10].
    16→- **Jurisdictional Adherence**: Follow state-specific evidentiary rules; video substitutable for live testimony with judge/party agreement, especially experts[1][2][6].
