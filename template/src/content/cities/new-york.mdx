New York City is one of the largest legal markets in the United States, home to thousands of law firms ranging from solo practitioners to global Am Law 100 firms. With courts spanning the Southern and Eastern Districts of New York, plus New York State Supreme Court, demand for professional deposition videography services is consistently high.

## How to Choose a Deposition Videographer in New York

When selecting a deposition videographer in NYC, consider these factors:

- **CLVS Certification** — Look for videographers holding the Certified Legal Video Specialist designation from NCRA
- **Multi-camera capability** — Complex depositions may require multiple angles
- **Real-time streaming** — Essential for remote attorney participation
- **Video-transcript sync** — Synchronized video and text for trial presentation
- **Same-day delivery** — Some firms offer expedited turnaround

## What to Expect

Deposition videography in New York typically costs **$500–2,000+ per session**, depending on duration, equipment requirements, and turnaround time. Most videographers charge a half-day or full-day rate plus additional fees for expedited delivery, streaming, and copies.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a certified videographer for depositions in New York?**
While New York does not require CLVS certification by statute, many courts and attorneys prefer certified videographers to ensure admissibility and professional standards.

**Can deposition video be used at trial in New York?**
Yes. Under CPLR 3113 and Federal Rules of Civil Procedure Rule 30(b)(3), videotaped depositions are admissible when properly noticed and recorded.
