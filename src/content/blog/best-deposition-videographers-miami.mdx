## What Makes Miami Different

South Florida's legal videography market has characteristics that separate it from Houston, New York, or Chicago.

**IME and medical examination work is a major segment.** Florida's personal injury and insurance litigation volume means independent medical examinations are routine, and video documentation of these exams is frequently the most contested evidence in a case. ACTION LEGAL VIDEO and Brickell Key both emphasize IME videography as a core service — not a sideline. If your firm handles PI work in Miami-Dade or Broward, your videographer needs specific experience documenting medical examinations with the kind of multi-angle coverage that holds up under scrutiny.

**Site inspection and evidence documentation.** Miami's real estate, construction, and insurance markets generate constant demand for professional video documentation of property damage, vehicle damage, and crime scenes. This is not deposition work per se, but the same videographers who handle depositions often handle these assignments — and the legal protocol requirements (chain of custody, proper documentation, admissibility standards) are identical. Having one provider who can handle both depositions and site inspections under a single relationship streamlines your vendor management considerably.

**Geographic sprawl across two coasts.** South Florida litigation does not stay in downtown Miami. Cases routinely involve depositions and inspections from Naples to Tampa on the Gulf Coast and from Homestead to Palm Beach on the Atlantic side. A provider with genuine regional coverage — not just a Miami address — saves you from coordinating multiple vendors with different quality standards and workflows. South Florida distances are deceptive: Brickell to Fort Lauderdale is only 30 miles but can eat 90 minutes in traffic.

**Cross-border and multilingual considerations.** Miami's legal market includes a significant volume of cases involving Latin American parties, international business disputes, and cross-border litigation. While language capability is primarily a court reporter concern, videographers working these depositions need to be comfortable with interpreted proceedings, which have different pacing, longer sessions, and specific protocol requirements that a general-purpose videographer will not anticipate.

> **Pro Tip:** When hiring for IME documentation, confirm that the videographer uses multi-camera setups and can provide close-up angles of the examination. A single wide shot from across the room may technically capture the event, but it will not show the specific manipulations, tests, and observations that become the crux of disputes over IME findings. The difference between a usable IME video and a useless one is almost always about camera angles, not camera quality.

## What to Demand From a Miami Videographer

Based on what actually matters in this market:

**In-house post-production and transcript sync.** The majority of value in deposition videography happens after the cameras stop rolling. The videographer watches and blends footage from multiple cameras, synchronizes the result to the transcript, and delivers in a format your trial software can use. Brickell Key runs this through their own production team. If your videographer outsources post-production, you lose control over turnaround time and quality — and you add a link to the chain of custody that opposing counsel will probe.

**Multi-camera capability as standard.** Single-camera depositions are a liability. You need at least two angles — a primary on the witness and a secondary for wide context — with redundant audio capture. For IMEs, three cameras is not excessive. The post-production team blends these into a cohesive record that shows exactly what happened.

**Rapid turnaround.** Miami litigation moves fast, especially in PI and insurance cases where depositions feed directly into mediation and settlement negotiations. ACTION LEGAL VIDEO emphasizes rapid delivery to attorneys and experts. Get a specific commitment on turnaround — 72 hours should be the ceiling for standard work, with same-day or next-day options for urgent matters.

**Proper understanding of legal protocol.** This sounds obvious, but it eliminates more candidates than you would expect. Your videographer needs to understand how to go on and off the record, how to handle objections during recording, chain-of-custody documentation, and how to swear in a witness on camera. Videographers are not typically required to be licensed in Florida, but insurance, professional standing, and demonstrated legal experience are essential screening criteria.

## Red Flags for the Miami Market

Watch for these specifically in South Florida:

- **Event videography background with no legal clients** — the protocol gap is real and will show up at the worst possible time
- **No IME or site inspection experience** in a market where these services are constant demand
- **Single-camera, single-angle setup** for depositions or medical examinations
- **No in-house post-production** — outsourced editing adds time, cost, and chain-of-custody risk
- **Miami-only coverage** when your cases span Broward, Palm Beach, or the Gulf Coast
- **No written agreement** covering deliverable formats, turnaround time, and cancellation terms
