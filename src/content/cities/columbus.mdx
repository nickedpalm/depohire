Columbus is Ohio's capital, its largest city, and the home of enough law firms along East Broad Street to keep a deposition videographer busy every day of the week. But busy doesn't mean easy to hire — the range between a polished professional and someone who'll hand you a thumb drive of unusable footage is wider than you'd think. Whether your deposition is at a firm downtown, a medical office in Dublin, or a corporate campus in Westerville, getting the video right matters more than most attorneys realize until it's too late.

## How to Choose a Deposition Videographer in Columbus

Columbus has a solid pool of legal video professionals, but you need to know what separates the good ones from the adequate:

- **CLVS certification is your filter.** Ohio courts accept video depositions under Rule 30(B)(3) of the Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure, but admissibility hinges on technical quality. A Certified Legal Video Specialist understands the procedural and technical requirements that protect your record. Start here and don't compromise.

- **Franklin County familiarity.** The Franklin County Court of Common Pleas is one of the busiest trial courts in Ohio. Videographers who regularly work this jurisdiction understand local expectations for video quality, format, and delivery. Ask how many depositions they've recorded in Franklin County in the past year.

- **Central Ohio coverage.** Columbus sprawls, and your deposition might land anywhere from the Short North to New Albany to Grove City. Confirm coverage for the full metro area, including the suburban rings where many corporate and medical depositions take place, without excessive travel fees.

- **Insurance litigation experience.** Columbus is home to major insurance carriers — Nationwide, State Auto, and several others have headquarters or major offices here. If you're handling insurance defense or bad faith litigation, a videographer experienced with these cases will understand the specific exhibit handling and multi-party deposition complexities involved.

- **Transcript synchronization standards.** The video must sync with the court reporter's transcript at the timecode level. Ask your videographer what sync software they use and whether they coordinate directly with your reporter. Sloppy sync creates expensive headaches during trial prep.

> **Pro Tip:** Ohio State's Moritz College of Law and Capital University Law School produce a steady stream of young attorneys in Columbus, many of whom are handling video depositions for the first time. If you're that attorney, ask the videographer to walk you through the process before the deposition starts. Good operators expect this and won't judge — they'd rather explain the procedure than fix problems on the record.

## What to Expect

Deposition videography in Columbus typically costs **$500–$1,300 per session**, placing it on the affordable end for a major metro. Half-day rates generally fall between $350 and $700. Expect additional charges for travel outside the I-270 outerbelt ($50–$150), real-time streaming ($100–$200), expedited delivery ($150–$400), and extra copies of the final media.

Setup begins 30–45 minutes before the deposition. The videographer positions cameras, checks audio levels, and tests lighting — conference room fluorescents are the enemy of good video, and experienced operators bring supplemental lighting to compensate. They open the record with the required statement, manage timestamps through breaks, and close the record at the end. Standard delivery takes 5–7 business days; rush turnaround is available when trial is bearing down.

> **Reality Check:** Columbus rates look attractive compared to Cleveland or Cincinnati, but don't mistake lower prices for lower stakes. A poorly lit, badly synced video deposition is just as damaging to your case in Franklin County as it would be anywhere else. The $200 you save on the cheapest option can easily turn into $2,000 in re-deposition costs if the footage is unusable.

## Local Market Overview

Columbus's legal market benefits from the city's role as Ohio's state capital and its concentration of insurance, banking, and healthcare corporate headquarters. The Southern District of Ohio's Columbus Division handles a heavy civil docket, and the Franklin County courts maintain one of the state's busiest litigation calendars — all of which keeps demand for competent deposition videography consistently strong.
