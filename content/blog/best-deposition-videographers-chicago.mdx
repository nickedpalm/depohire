A defense attorney I know in the Loop told me about the deposition that changed how his firm vets videographers. They were recording a complex medical device liability case — multiple experts, three days of testimony, opposing counsel flying in from out of state. The videographer they hired had solid reviews online and a reasonable rate. Day one went fine. Day two, the power in the conference room flickered during a critical cross-examination. The videographer had no backup recording device. Thirty-eight minutes of testimony — gone. Not corrupted, not recoverable. Gone. The court reporter had the transcript, but without the synchronized video showing the expert witness visibly contradicting their own written report, the footage that would have made the jury sit up — the case lost a weapon it could not get back.

I'll be honest: Chicago's deposition videography market does not get enough attention. East Coast and West Coast firms dominate the industry conversation, but the Midwest legal market is massive, the standards are high, and the pricing is more transparent than what you will find in New York or LA. The problem is that the pool of dedicated legal videographers in Chicago is smaller than the market deserves, which means the gap between the best and the rest is wide.

Here's what most people miss: Chicago's strength is not volume of options — it is the quality of the firms that have committed to this niche full-time.

## The Remote Deposition Shift

Chicago has leaned into remote and hybrid depositions harder than some markets, and Urlaub Bowen is a good example of why. The firm offers free Zoom support, tech tutorials for attorneys unfamiliar with the platform, and real-time streaming to PCs and tablets at no extra charge. Their approach — making remote depositions frictionless rather than charging a premium for the technology — has been a significant draw for Illinois firms adjusting to post-pandemic workflows.

This matters because the remote vs. in-person decision is not just about cost. For a standard witness deposition in a straightforward case, a competent remote setup captures everything you need. For anything where witness demeanor is critical — hostile witnesses, emotional testimony, expert credibility challenges — in-person recording with a professional videographer in the room remains the standard.

## What to Verify Before Hiring in Chicago

After researching this market, here are the specific things to confirm with any Chicago-area videographer:

**Synchronized video-text delivery.** Capital Reporting uses DVD, MPEG1, and MPEG4 formats with synchronized text and hyperlinked exhibits. That last part — hyperlinked exhibits — is a feature that speeds up trial prep enormously. Ask whether your videographer's deliverable includes exhibit linking or just raw video.

**Real-time reporting capability.** For complex depositions, real-time transcript streaming to your tablet while the deposition is happening lets you adjust your questioning on the fly. Both Capital Reporting and Urlaub Bowen offer this.

**Suburban and collar county coverage.** If your depositions extend beyond the Loop into the suburbs, confirm coverage and travel fees. Urlaub Bowen explicitly covers Chicago and surrounding areas, but not every firm does.

**Backup and redundancy protocols.** The power-flicker story I opened with is not hypothetical — it is a recurring risk in older Chicago buildings. Every firm you hire should be running dual-recording systems with independent power backup.
